### FX Receivable Exposure and Hedging Considerations

To: CFO
From: Finance Team
Subject: Managing EUR Receivable Exposure

Our firm is expected to receive €12,500,000 in one year from a European client. This creates a foreign exchange (FX) receivable exposure, as the ultimate U.S. dollar value of this payment will depend on the EUR/USD exchange rate at the time of conversion.

The key risk is that the euro depreciates relative to the U.S. dollar over the next year. If the EUR weakens, each euro received will translate into fewer U.S. dollars, reducing total revenue and potentially compressing margins. Given the size of this receivable, even modest exchange rate movements could result in a material variance in cash inflows. This introduces uncertainty into financial planning and exposes the firm to risks that are unrelated to its core operations.

To manage this exposure, we can consider three primary hedging approaches. A forward contract would allow us to lock in a fixed exchange rate today, eliminating uncertainty and ensuring predictable cash flows. However, this approach removes any upside if the euro strengthens. A money market hedge provides a synthetic forward by borrowing euros, converting to dollars, and investing domestically. While effective, it is more operationally complex and depends on prevailing interest rate conditions. Finally, FX options offer downside protection while preserving upside potential. By purchasing a put option on euros, we can establish a minimum USD value while still benefiting if the euro appreciates. The trade-off is the upfront premium cost.

Given the scale of the exposure and current market volatility, hedging is worth considering to stabilize cash flows and reduce earnings variability. The optimal strategy will depend on our risk tolerance and cost considerations.

As next steps, we will build an Excel-based model to quantify hedge outcomes under various exchange rate scenarios. We will then document the model design and assumptions and use these results to recommend a specific hedging strategy aligned with the firm’s financial objectives.
